Education

Elon Musk's school days were challenging, as he faced bullying and even ended up hospitalized after being beaten by bullies who threw him down a set of stairs. This incident reportedly occurred after Musk made insensitive comments to a classmate. Musk has mentioned feeling out of sync with his classmates and has spoken about the social challenges he faced, stating that social cues were not intuitive for him. In 2021, he revealed that he has Asperger's syndrome during his appearance on "Saturday Night Live."

During his childhood, Musk developed a passion for reading and coding, dedicating many late nights to these activities. At the age of 17, he achieved an exceptional score on a university-level aptitude test for computer programming skills. After graduating from high school, he moved to Canada with his family and studied at Queen's University in Ontario before completing his education at the University of Pennsylvania, earning degrees in physics and economics.

While at the University of Pennsylvania, Musk and a classmate transformed a 10-bedroom frat house into a nightclub, showcasing one of his early entrepreneurial endeavors. After completing his undergraduate studies, Musk briefly pursued a Ph.D. at Stanford University but left after only two days to seize opportunities during the emerging dot-com boom. He never returned to Stanford to complete his doctoral program.

A Journey of Innovation and Ambition

Driven by the belief that humanity must become a multiplanetary species, Musk founded SpaceX in 2002 to develop more affordable rockets. The company successfully launched the Falcon 1 in 2006, followed by the Falcon 9 in 2010. SpaceX's Falcon Heavy, launched in 2018, was designed to carry significantly more payload than its competitors at a lower cost. Musk introduced the Super Heavy-Starship system as the successor to the Falcon rockets, aiming to enable fast transportation on Earth and establish bases on the Moon and Mars. SpaceX also created the Dragon spacecraft, which transports supplies and astronauts to the International Space Station (ISS). In 2020, Dragon completed a crewed flight to the ISS with NASA astronauts on board. SpaceX conducted test flights of the Super Heavy-Starship system in 2020, and Musk played a key role as CEO and chief designer in the development of Falcon rockets, Dragon, and Starship. The company is contracted by NASA to build the lunar lander for the Artemis space program, with a target date of 2025 for astronauts to return to the Moon.

Revolutionizing Electric Cars and Proposing the Hyperloop

Elon Musk's interest in electric cars led him to become a major funder of Tesla Motors (later renamed Tesla) in 2004. Tesla introduced its first car, the Roadster, in 2006, which was a high-performance electric sports car capable of traveling 245 miles on a single charge. Tesla's innovative approach to electric vehicles challenged the perception of them being dull and unexciting. The company went public in 2010, raising around $226 million, and continued to make waves in the automotive industry with the introduction of the acclaimed Model S sedan in 2012, followed by the Model X luxury SUV in 2015. In 2017, Tesla started production of the more affordable Model 3, which became the best-selling electric car of all time. In 2013, dissatisfied with the projected cost of a high-speed rail system in California, Musk proposed an alternative concept called the Hyperloop. The Hyperloop was envisioned as a high-speed transportation system where passenger pods would travel through pneumatic tubes, enabling rapid travel between Los Angeles and San Francisco in just 35 minutes at speeds close to the speed of sound. Musk estimated the Hyperloop's cost to be significantly lower than the proposed rail system, but due to his commitments with SpaceX and Tesla, he was unable to personally pursue its development.

From Controversial Tweets to a Bid for Twitter Ownership

Elon Musk joined Twitter in 2009 and quickly gained a massive following, with over 85 million followers by 2022. In 2018, he tweeted about potentially taking Tesla private at a value of $420 per share, which led to legal issues. The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) sued Musk for securities fraud, resulting in a settlement where he stepped down as Tesla's chairman for three years, but remained CEO. Musk expressed criticism towards Twitter's content moderation policies and in 2022, he made a bid to acquire the entire company. Initially accepted, Musk later withdrew his bid, claiming Twitter had breached the purchase agreement. Twitter sued Musk, and ultimately, its shareholders voted to accept his offer, leading to the completion of the deal in October 2022.

OpenAI

In 2015, Elon Musk co-founded OpenAI, a nonprofit organization focused on researching artificial intelligence and ensuring its safe and beneficial development. However, he stepped down from the board later to avoid potential conflicts of interest with Tesla, which was making advancements in AI for its self-driving car technology. Despite this, Musk has expressed criticism towards OpenAI and is now working on launching his own AI competitor, playfully named "Truth GPT," following the success of OpenAI's ChatGPT. During the same period, SpaceX, under Musk's leadership, achieved significant milestones, including 24 launches by the end of 2015. These launches included tasks such as resupplying the International Space Station and breaking records in the process. In 2015, Tesla also introduced its initial version of Autopilot, a driver-assist feature for its electric vehicles (EVs). Over the years, Musk continued to enhance this technology, eventually releasing an upgraded version called Full Self-Driving beta.
